Twitter is a [social networking](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_networking) and [micro-blogging](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micro-blogging) service that allows its users to send and read other users' updates (otherwise known as tweets), which are text-based posts of up to 140 characters in length.
Updates are displayed on the user's profile page and delivered to other users who have signed up to receive them. Senders can restrict delivery to those in their circle of friends (delivery to everyone being the default). Users can receive updates via the Twitter website, [SMS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SMS), [RSS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RSS), or through applications such as [TwitterMobile](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TwitterMobile), [Tweetie](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tweetie), [Twinkle](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twinkle), [Twitterrific](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twitterrific), [Feedalizr](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feedalizr), [Facebook](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Facebook), and Twidget, a [widget](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Widget) application. Four gateway numbers are currently available for SMS: [short codes](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Short_codes) for the [United States](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States), [Canada](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada), and [India](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India), and a [United Kingdom](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom)-based number for international use. Several third parties offer posting and receiving updates via email. By September 2008, Twitter had 'well over 5 million visitors.'[1]
Twitter messages may be [tagged](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tag_%28metadata%29) using hashtags, a word or phrase prefixed with a #, such as #beer.
